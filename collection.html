03 / Philosophy

Designing for
Isolation.

The Environment

Every sanctuary in the collection is positioned at the intersection of raw nature and absolute silence. We seek out landscapes that command respect, forcing the architecture to yield to the topography rather than conquer it.

The Materials

Constructed from locally sourced elements—rammed earth, Valser quartzite, charred timber—the structures are designed to weather and age, developing a patina that eventually camouflages them completely into their surroundings.

The Climate

Thermal mass and passive ventilation replace artificial climate control wherever possible. The architecture acts as a thermal battery, storing daytime heat and releasing it during the alpine night.
